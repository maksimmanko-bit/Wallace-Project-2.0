MAXM Manual

Help & Instructions

Step-by-step guidance for every section of the portal.

Overview

Getting Started

MAXM is a secure shared workspace for receiving, shipping, customer pickups, trailers, warranty claims, panel inspections, stock remaining, files, photos, and reports.

Check the connection first

Confirm that the header says Server Connected before creating or changing a record.

  1. Sign in once.The site remembers a trusted device for up to one month, so daily work stays fast.
  2. Use the menu button.Help, theme, account actions, and settings are grouped under the top-right menu.
  3. Select the correct tab.Use the physical movement or task to decide where the record belongs.
  4. Search before creating.Search by PO, Job, BOL, customer, supplier, or another known reference to avoid duplicates.
  5. Open an existing record.Click the underlined PO Number, Job Number, or BOL # to read the complete record.
  6. Edit instead of recreating.Use the pencil button to change an existing record, then save the form.
Incoming Freight

Receiving

Use Receiving for goods ordered from suppliers and delivered to the warehouse.

RequiredPO Number, Supplier, Ordered By, Date & Time Ordered, Classification.
OptionalDescription, ETA, Courier, Tracking Number, Initial Status, Notes, Packing Slip.

Create an incoming shipment

  1. Click New Receiving.Enter PO Number, Supplier, Ordered By, ordered date and Classification.
  2. Add useful optional information.Description, ETA, courier, tracking number, notes, and packing slip make the record easier to find and verify.
  3. Click Save Shipment.The new record appears in Receiving Logs with status Ordered unless another status was selected.

Receive the shipment

  1. Click Receive.Select the employee, confirm the date and time, and upload POD files or photos.
  2. Inspect before the driver leaves.If anything is damaged, short, missing, or incorrect, enable the OSD option.
  3. Document OSD.Describe the problem, take clear photos of freight, packaging and labels, and follow the courier instructions shown on screen.
Never sign clean when an OSD issue is visible

Record the exception on the driver paperwork and keep packaging available for inspection.

ETA and status

ETA is optional and helps highlight overdue freight. Incoming statuses are Ordered, Received, and Canceled. Received is a completed status.

Outbound Freight

Shipped Out

Use Shipped Out when freight leaves the warehouse for a customer, job site, or another destination.

RequiredCustomer, Courier, Payment Type, Ship To Address.
OptionalJob Number, Notes, outbound condition photos.
  1. Click New Shipped Out.Customer, Courier, Ship To Address, and Payment Type are required.
  2. Add Job Number and notes.Job Number becomes the clickable reference in the first table column.
  3. Add condition photos.Photograph the freight before it leaves so its outbound condition can be proven later.
  4. Edit from the pencil button.Saving an edit updates the same record and does not create a second shipment.

Payment Type options are Collect, Pre-Paid, and Wallace Covered. Add a tracking number when available to open the courier tracking page from the record.

Customer Handoff

Customer Pick Up

Use this section when a customer collects freight directly from the warehouse.

RequiredJob Number, Handled By, at least one pickup photo.
OptionalCustomer Name and comments.
  1. Click New Pick Up.Enter the required Job Number and select the employee who released the freight.
  2. Add customer and comments when useful.These fields are optional but help identify the handoff.
  3. Upload at least one photo.A handoff photo is required before the record can be saved.
  4. Open details from Job Number.The complete record and photos are available from the first column.

Use pickup photos to prove the freight condition and handoff at the time the customer collected it.

Trailer Movement

Trailers

Trailer Logs track the full movement from Expected through Arrived and Dispatched.

Required and optional information

RequiredTrip #, BOL #, Stop #, Status. Issue Description becomes required when Report an issue is enabled.
OptionalWeight in lb, Trailer #, supporting PDF/Excel documents, Issue photos.
  1. Expected.Create the record and attach searchable PDF or Excel supporting documents.
  2. Arrived.Change the status when the trailer arrives. The current date and time are recorded automatically, and the Email button prepares an arrival email.
  3. Dispatched.Change the status when the trailer leaves. The current date and time are recorded automatically.
  4. Review elapsed time.Trailer Logs show how long passed between Arrived and Dispatched.

Click BOL # to open the full record. Use Report an issue to add a description and photos. After selecting new Issue photos, click Annotate to draw arrows, rectangles, text, or highlights before saving.

Claims

Warranty

Use Warranty to create and manage warranty service claims from the first report through approval or denial.

Base fields

RequiredJob Number, Reported Issue, Order Type, Claim Type, Status. Progress and decision statuses add their own required fields.
OptionalWarranty photos while the claim is To be Claimed.

Order Type is Install, Supply, or Stock. Claim Type is Damage/Defect, Shortage/Missing, or Other.

To be ClaimedDefault status. Record the issue and claim classification.
Working on itAdd Original Order ID, Customer Name, and Warranty Notes.
Request SubmittedKeep the original order, customer, and submission notes current.
ApprovedAdd Original Order ID, Customer Name, Warranty Notes, and Approved ID.
DeniedAdd Original Order ID, Customer Name, Warranty Notes, and Denied Reason.

Click Job Number for the full claim. The Dashboard summarizes warranty volume by period, status, and claim type.

Panel Quality

Panel Inspections

Use Panel Inspections to request a panel check and record the final inspection result.

Inspection RequestCustomer Name, Job Number, Status, and Requested By are required. Notes are optional.
Completed inspectionInspected By and at least one photo are required. Add Notes when damage is found.
Inspection RequestEnter Customer Name, Job Number, Notes, and select Requested By from the shared employee list.
Inspected - good to goSelect Inspected By and upload at least one required inspection photo.
Inspected - damage foundSelect Inspected By, describe findings in Notes, and upload required evidence photos.

The available inspectors are Alex, Brian, Maksym, and Valerii. Completed inspection statuses are final workflow states.

Inventory View

Stock Remaining

Use Stock Remaining to check door, operator, rail, and extra item availability without opening separate spreadsheets.

Main viewShows item name, category, sold count, stock level, remaining quantity, and alert status.
AnalyticsHighlights Doors Sold, Operators Sold, Doors Alert, Operators Alert, and Rails & Extra Items Alert.
  1. Open Stock Remaining.The table loads current stock records from the shared database.
  2. Use search and filters.Find stock by item name, category, status, or alert condition.
  3. Open a stock card.Click the main stock reference to review full details in the same style as other records.
  4. Watch low-stock blocks.Alert blocks help employees spot items that need attention before a shortage becomes a workflow problem.
Operations

Dashboard

Open Dashboard from the top menu to review activity for Today, Week, Month, or Year.

  • Received, Shipped Out, and Picked Up totals.
  • Current Trailer Holding Time for trailers that are Arrived but not yet Dispatched.
  • OSD incident count and incidents by courier.
  • Average time between order and receipt.
  • Average Trailer Holding Time between Arrived and Dispatched.
  • Overdue and upcoming ETA notifications.
  • Warranty claim count, claims by status, and claims by type.
Evidence & Retrieval

Search, Files & Reports

Search and filters

Search applies to the active tab, and Global Search checks all sections at once, including Stock Remaining. Use status, courier, and active filter tags where available. Text-based PDF and Excel attachments are searchable.

Photos and documents

Hover photo buttons on desktop to preview images. Click Photos, POD, OSD, Docs, or Issue to launch the gallery. Use arrows to move between files, zoom or rotate images, annotate photos where available, and click Download to save the current file automatically.

Action times

Open any record, click Edit, and use the Action Times section to correct Ordered, Received, Shipped, Picked Up, Created, Arrived, Dispatched, Submitted, Approved, Denied, or Inspected timestamps available for that workflow. Saving the record updates the Timeline and Status Log.

PDF reports

Open a record by clicking its PO, Job, BOL, or main reference number, then click PDF Report inside the record card. The report includes saved information, Timeline where relevant, labeled evidence photos, attached files as additional pages when supported, and page numbers.

Excel exports

Use the Excel export button beside Sync Database to download the current table data from the active tab. Stock Remaining also has its own Excel export for Doors and Operators. Excel reports do not include photos or attachments.

Photo uploads are optimized automatically

Large image files are reduced before upload to improve speed while keeping useful evidence quality.

Access & Troubleshooting

Mobile & Support

The portal works in current desktop, iOS, and Android browsers. On phones, desktop tables become vertical record cards with no horizontal scrolling. Tap anywhere on a card to open its full-screen details.

Mobile controls

  • Use the bottom navigation to switch Receiving, Shipped Out, and Customer Pick Up.
  • Swipe the bottom navigation for Trailers, Warranty, Panel Inspections, and Stock Remaining.
  • The center + button creates a record in the active section.
  • Tap Filters beside Search to show or hide status and courier filters.
  • Photos and Next Action remain available directly in mobile cards.

If something does not work

  1. Check Server Connected.Wait for the connection before saving.
  2. Refresh the data.Use the circular refresh button above the table.
  3. Check required fields.Fields marked with an asterisk must be completed.
  4. Keep the page open during uploads.Large groups of photos need time to finish uploading.
  5. Search for the record after saving.Do not press Save repeatedly while waiting for the first save to complete.
